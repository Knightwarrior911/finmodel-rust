REMAINING GAPS AND EXPLANATIONS
========================================================================

  revenue (1 remaining: APA)
    APA Corp (CIK 1841666) is a 2021 holdco redomicile of Apache Corp. It files
    revenue under a custom extension tag not in us-gaap namespace. The original
    Apache Corp (CIK 6769) has revenue through 2020 via 'Revenues'. This is an
    insoluble gap for the XBRL approach for the holdco entity.

  cogs (24 remaining)
    All are financial companies or pure-asset-management firms (ARES, BLK, IVZ,
    BRO, ICE, KKR, etc.) for which COGS is structurally inapplicable. Their
    expenses are operating expenses (compensation, management fees, claims), not
    a COGS line. The derivation engine can proxy gross_profit via revenue - cogs
    when cogs is available; for these companies gross_profit is also N/A.

  gross_profit (234 remaining)
    ~46% of the S&P 500 (financials, insurers, utilities, software, consulting)
    do not report GrossProfit in XBRL. This is by design: GAAP does not require
    a gross profit subtotal for service businesses. Coverage cannot realistically
    exceed ~55% without introducing semantically incorrect proxies.
    The derivation engine computes gross_profit = revenue - cogs wherever possible.

  ebit (5 remaining: ARE, CPT, NVR, PFG, PHM)
    These are REITs (ARE, CPT) and homebuilders (NVR, PHM) that use
    IncomeLossFromContinuingOperationsBeforeIncomeTaxes or similar pre-tax income
    tags instead of OperatingIncomeLoss. PFG is a financial services company.
    The new ebit fallback tags added cover most such cases; these 5 are edge
    cases where the operating/pre-tax income concept is filed under company-
    specific extension tags.

  da (2 remaining: EG, PCG)
    Everest Group (EG, insurer) and PG&E (PCG, utility) appear to file D&A
    as part of other line items without a standalone DepreciationDepletionAndAmortization
    tag. EG is an insurance holding co with minimal tangible assets;
    PCG emerged from bankruptcy in 2020 and has irregular filings.

  capex (23 remaining)
    All are financial companies (BAC, JPM, BAC, AFL, CB, etc.) for which
    physical asset capex is immaterial and not tagged separately, or energy/O&G
    companies using PaymentsToExploreAndDevelopOilAndGasProperties (E&P capex)
    rather than PaymentsToAcquirePropertyPlantAndEquipment.

  accounts_receivable (41 remaining)
    Mix of: (1) financial cos reporting loans-held-for-investment rather than
    receivables (COF, BAC, WFC); (2) insurance cos with premium receivables
    under different tags; (3) asset-light companies (ABNB, ACN, ADSK) where
    receivables appear in notes disclosures only.

  inventory (162 remaining)
    ~32% of S&P 500 are pure service/financial companies with no inventory.
    ABNB, ACN, ADBE, ADP, ADSK, AFL, AIG, etc. — these are legitimate N/A cases.
    The 162 includes 100+ companies where inventory is correctly absent.

  accounts_payable (42 remaining)
    Financial companies (BAC, BK, C, GS, JPM, WFC, etc.) that report
    "payables to brokers/dealers" or "deposits" rather than trade payables.
    These are structurally different balance sheet items.

  ppe_net (13 remaining)
    Includes financial holding companies (ACGL, APO, ARES, BRK-B) with
    minimal fixed assets, and NVR (homebuilder that leases rather than owns
    land — unusual business model with very low PPE).

  long_term_debt (18 remaining)
    Mix of: (1) debt-free companies (EXPD, GRMN, ISRG, LULU, ALGN, DECK);
    (2) financial companies (AFL, BRK-B) using insurance-specific debt tags;
    (3) new/small-cap adjacent companies (HOOD, COIN) with limited XBRL history.

  eps_diluted (8 remaining)
    BRK-B uses Class A/B share structure with non-standard EPS reporting.
    ARES and KKR are partnerships with per-unit rather than per-share EPS.
    ERIE uses a single-share-class filing convention. STZ, V, HSY, ABNB
    appear to have EPS under slightly different tag names in some filing years.

  shares_diluted (4 remaining: BKR, ERIE, STZ, V)
    BKR and ERIE use non-standard share count tags. STZ and V have
    filing-period mismatches where diluted shares appear in quarterly
    (10-Q) but not consistently in annual (10-K FY) tagged periods.

  least ONE annual 10-K/20-F FY-period entry in companyfacts JSON.
- This is a presence check, not a continuity check. A company with only
  one year of data (vs. the 3+ years the model requests) will show as
  "covered" here but may still fail at model-generation time.
- Rate limit: 0.12s delay between EDGAR requests (~8 req/sec).
